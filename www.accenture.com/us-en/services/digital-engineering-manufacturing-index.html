Reimagine products to control costs and design for resilience

As businesses navigate ongoing volatility, the costs of refining product designs and increasing inventory to remain competitive have spiralled. Embedding a digital thread through the entire value chain is key to building resiliency and optimizing costs.

  1. Digital twins for automation and resilience
  2. Digital intelligence to bridge operational gaps
  3. Driving efficiency with warehouse automation
  4. The second wave of the global supply chain
  5. How supply chain visibility delivers resilience

Many manufacturers encounter two roadblocks in adopting automation: They have to apply it across multiple assets, lines and functions, and their existing manufacturing operations management (MOM) architectures can't accommodate the vast amounts of data required.

Digital twins simulate the operating behavior of physical assets and processes, storing and contextualizing data from multiple sources in a way that engineers, operators and decision-makers understand to help improve decision-making.

How many companies believe they can grow without changing their manufacturing footprints?

Virtually none: 1 percent, to be specific. Accenture's survey of more than 1,000 executives at large companies reveals change is not only in the air, it's the only option.

Businesses are making moves. A stunning 94 percent of North American companies are planning direct investment in reshoring, signalling a shift from surviving disruption to rethinking how their supply chain foundations drive resilience and competitive advantage in future scenarios.

Supply chains are no longer cost centers; they're engines for growth that can positively impact business, society and the planet. Take a closer look into how the second wave of the global supply chain is shaping up.

The first step to managing disruption is understanding how it affects your business. To do that, you need two types of visibility into your supply chain networks: structural and dynamic visibility.

Structural visibility is a snapshot of your operations, logistics routes, potential weaknesses, etc. Dynamic visibility looks at product status, facility performance and the various ways in which disruptions impact them.

Together, they comprise what we call "intelligent visibility," which helps businesses control costs, inefficiencies, and complexity to improve competitiveness in the short and long-term.

Frequently asked questions

Digital engineering services utilize data and transformative technologies to help organizations to design, develop and launch software-enabled devices, equipment, products and services for today’s discerning consumers. Alongside digitizing their engineering process to make seismic changes across their product and operating value chain to optimize and transform traditional R&D and engineering processes, tools and methods that have existed for decades. By combining our deep digital engineering solutions expertise, innovation networks and advanced technologies such as digital twins, robotics, AR/VR, cloud, data, AI, we can help you reimagine the products you make and how you make them.

The benefits of digital engineering are plentiful, as it plays a critical role in any new initiatives. And by digitizing the engineering process companies can increase returns on R&D investments; unlock new revenue streams; better optimize existing product portfolios; accelerate time to market; improve product reliability; and increase consumer confidence and loyalty through secure and sustainable products.

Digital manufacturing services utilize data and transformative technologies to help forward-looking organizations digitize and optimize their manufacturing sites at scale, enabling them to produce modern, intelligent and sustainable products that are fit for today’s digitally savvy consumers and omnichannel marketplace. This involves shaking up traditional manufacturing operations to become more customer-centric, agile and responsive. Digital manufacturing services have been revolutionized by today’s technology – allowing organizations to transform the way projects are planned, managed and executed by building smart factories of the future that leverage 5G technology and put advanced robotics and warehouse automation at their core.

The benefits of digital manufacturing include: improved production and operations; greater capital efficiencies; reduced manufacturing costs and environmental impact; improved safety, productivity and experiences for the industrial worker; and enhanced cyber resilience and operational availability.
